How to thrive here

Three habits that make this course work for you.

01

Keep the pace

This course is online and self-paced within each week. A little each day beats cramming. Follow the weekly schedule and you stay ahead.

02

Reason, don't memorize

Physiology is mechanism. Ask why at every step. Understanding how a system works transfers to the exam and the clinic. Memorized lists fade.

03

Do the labs by hand

Each week's dry lab is worked by hand: calculations, graphs, and predictions from real data. Given, not Googled. The hand work is how it sticks.

How each week works

A steady rhythm, every week.

01

Prepare

Open the week and work through the concept material before the deadline. You build the idea before you apply it.

02

Work the dry lab

Each week has a case-based dry lab: calculate, plot, and predict from data, all by hand. Photograph your pages and upload.

03

Check yourself

A short retrieval quiz closes each week so you know what landed and what needs another pass.

04

Exams and a final

Three unit exams (Weeks 4, 7, and 11) and one cumulative final at the end of the term. Dates are in your syllabus.

Questions, answered

What students usually want to know first.

What should I do first?+

Read the syllabus, open Week 1, and work the first dry lab. That is all you need to start strong.

It's online. When do I work?+

The course is asynchronous and self-paced within each week. There are no live meetings. Follow the weekly schedule and meet the deadlines, and you can work at the times that fit your life.

How do exams work?+

Three unit exams fall in Weeks 4, 7, and 11, and a cumulative final closes the term. Your weekly dry labs and short retrieval checks count too. Full weights and dates are in your syllabus.

Do the labs really have to be by hand?+

Yes. The dry labs are worked by hand: calculations, plotted graphs, and written predictions from data. Hand work is the integrity mechanism for this course. Photograph or scan your pages and upload them.

Where do I get help when I'm stuck?+

Start with the weekly module and the schedule. Office hours and contact details are in your syllabus. You are never stuck alone in this course.

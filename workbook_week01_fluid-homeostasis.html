Case 02 · Patient: Kofi M., age 24 (fictional)

Kofi returns from travel with sudden, profuse, watery diarrhea, painless and almost clear ("rice-water" stool). He has passed an estimated 1 liter every hour for the last 8 hours. He is thirsty, lightheaded when he stands, and his heart is racing. Baseline healthy body mass: 70 kg. Over the next three weeks you will study Kofi at three levels: this week the whole body and its fluids, next week the membrane transport that is driving the loss, and in Week 3 the electrical consequences of what he is losing.

Part 1 of 4 · Calculate

Map Kofi's body fluid compartments

Before you can judge how sick Kofi is, you need to know how much water he started with and where it lives. Use the standard estimates below for a healthy adult.

Given (use these rules)

  • Total body water (TBW) ≈ 60% of body mass (1 kg water = 1 L).
  • Of TBW: intracellular fluid (ICF) = 2/3; extracellular fluid (ECF) = 1/3.

Part 3 of 4 · Interpret

Find the feedback loops

Kofi's body is fighting back. Two regulated variables are under threat: blood pressure (falling as plasma drains) and plasma osmolality (rising as he loses watery fluid). For each, a negative feedback loop is trying to defend the setpoint.

Label a negative feedback loop

Choose the falling-blood-pressure response. Draw the loop and label all five parts: the regulated variable, the sensor, the integrating center, the effector, and the response. Add an arrow showing why the loop is "negative" (the response opposes the change).

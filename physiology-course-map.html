Human Physiology Course Map

Fall 2026 · September 8 to December 11 · Online, asynchronous with lab

A 14-week sequence built around high-yield, mechanism-level physiology. Topics build from cell and membrane foundations through excitable tissue, the organ systems, and integrative control. Each week pairs a lecture focus with an asynchronous lab. Depth sits between an introductory survey and a graduate or professional course: you reason through mechanisms, not just name structures.

  • Length14 weeks
  • Units8 units, 14 labs
  • Unit examsWeeks 4, 7, 11
  • Final (cumulative)Finals week, Dec 7 to 11

Sequencing note: this map places the endocrine unit after the renal and digestive units so that glucose, fluid, and metabolic regulation reinforce one another. An alternative high-yield arrangement pairs the endocrine unit immediately after the nervous system, treating neural and hormonal signaling as the two control systems before the organ-system tour. Either order works; choose based on how your assessments are weighted.
